Protect Your Basement From Water Damage

Flooded basements are costly. Not only are the valuable possesions you have stored damanged, but mold and bacteria can grow in the wood and cause quality issues that often lead to bigger problems.

The risk of flooding is most severe during the spring when the snow melts while there is still frost in the ground, or when there's so much rain the ground canâ€™t absorb the water quick enough and the water flows to the lowest point. Luckily there are ways for you to better protect your basement from flooding.

One way to do prevent basement flooding is by installing a sump pump slightly below the cellar floor, which is triggered by a float switch whenever the water level rises to just below your foundation. When triggered, the excess water is pumped away from your home through piping. You'll need to be able to get power to the pump and have enough piping take the water away far enough where it won't seep back towards your foundation.

There are other methods that help prevent basement flooding. Installing a french drain is one of those methods, although they are less popular because they require more planning and work to install. Which makes a sump pump an even more viable option to keep your basement safe.
